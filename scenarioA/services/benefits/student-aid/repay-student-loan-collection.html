What happens if you miss a payment

If you think that you will have trouble making a payment, you can first try to reduce the amount you pay each month.

  • Change your monthly payment

You can also look into the types of repayment assistance we offer:

  • Get repayment assistance

Missing a payment will have a negative impact on your credit rating.

Contact us to discuss options

Even if you have already missed a loan payment, you still have options. Contact us to discuss options to help you get back on track.

  • If you live in Ontario, British Columbia, Saskatchewan, New Brunswick, or Newfoundland and Labrador

    Contact the National Student Loans Service Centre.

  • If you live in Alberta, Manitoba, Nova Scotia or Prince Edward Island

    You have provincial and federal parts of your loan that need to be looked at seprately separately.

    Contact both your provincial loan provider and the National Student Loans Service Centre.

  • If you live in Quebec, Nunavut or the Northwest Territories

    Contact directly to your province or territory.

    • Getting your loan out of collection

    If you missed payments on your Canada Student Loan for 270 days or more (9 months), your loan is considered to be in default and is sent to the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) for collection. Canada Student Loan rehabilitation is an opportunity to have your loan returned to the National Student Loans Service Centre (NSLSC). To find out if your loan can be rehabilitated, contact the NSLSC .

    Note: If you have a judgment or legal action on your account, you may not be eligible for Canada Student Loan rehabilitation. Contact the NSLSC and ask to speak with a Canada Student Loans Program (CSLP) client relations officer to discuss your situation.

    For the provincial portion of your student loan, contact the student loan provider in your province of permanent residence for more information.

    Rehabilitate your Canada Student Loan

    If you are eligible for Canada Student Loan rehabilitation, you must contact the NSLSC to:

    • arrange a repayment schedule with the CRA;
    • repay all interest owed on your Canada Student Loan; and
    • make the equivalent of two monthly payments on the loan (the agreed monthly payment that is set out in your repayment schedule with CRA). CRA may also allow you to make a lump sum payment combining the two monthly payments and the interest owed.

    Once you have completed your payments, contact the NSLSC and ask to speak with a CSLP client relations officer to inform them that you have fulfilled the requirements to rehabilitate your loan.

    Contact NSLSC

    If you have not received a decision letter from the CSLP within 20 business days, call the NSLSC and ask to speak with a CSLP client relations officer about your loan rehabilitation.

    Bankruptcy doesn't erase student loans during the first 7 years

    Be aware that if you file for bankruptcy within seven years of finishing your studies, your Canada Student Loan won’t be discharged as part of the bankruptcy. You’ll have to continue paying back your Canada Student Loan.
