Manage your student loan at the NSLSC

The National Student Loans Service Center (NSLSC) is where you manage your student loan.

On this page

  • What you can do in your NSLSC account
  • Update your banking information
  • Keep your loan interest free while you study
  • Paying back your student loan
  • Contact us

What you can do in your NSLSC account

  • Confirm your enrolment
  • Change your address
  • See your previous payments
  • Check your loan balance
  • View and customize your repayment options
  • Apply for repayment assistance
  • Get your tax forms
Login to your account

Update your banking information

You need to complete and send a paper form by mail to update your bank account.

Download the appropriate form below and mail it with a void cheque/direct deposit form from the bank to:

National Student Loans Service Centre (NSLSC)
P.O. Box 4030
Mississauga, ON L5A 4M4

  • Change the bank account in which you receive money from your student loan (PDF, 36 KB)
  • Change the bank account through which you repay your student loan (PDF, 128 KB)

Not yet signed your agreement

If you have not yet finished your application for your loan or grant and have not signed your Master Student Financial Assistance Agreement (MSFAA), you should update the banking information that appears on your Certificate of Eligibility and Canada Student Loan Agreement.

Keep your loan interest free while you study

Let us know you're still studying so that your loan repayment will be delayed until you finish, and remain interest free.

Taking out a new loan

If you are taking out a new loan, the National Student Loans Service Center (NSLSC) will be notified as part of the process.

Continue studying without a new loan

Check with your province to find out how to notify us. You might need to Login to your NSLSC account to submit a request for Confirmation of Enrolment.

Reservist status in the Canadian Forces

You can also delay repayment and interest on your student loan if you are a reservist in the Canadian Forces serving in a designated operation.

Download and complete the Confirmation of Posting Assignment for Full-Time Students form and submit it along with your loan application to maintain your interest-free status. Make sure you attach a copy of your notification of posting instructions that you received from the Department of National Defence.

If you need help with this, contact your provincial or territorial student financial assistance office.

Paying back your student loan

To find out when and how to start repaying, change payments and get help with repaying: Repay your student loan.

Contact us

If you have questions about student loans and grants, contact us.
