Provincial payments

  • Ontario, British Columbia, Saskatchewan, New Brunswick, or Newfoundland and Labrador

    You repay all your student loans through the National Student Loan Service Centre. Use the instructions on this page.

  • Alberta, Manitoba, Nova Scotia and Prince Edward Island

    You have provincial and federal parts of your loan that need to be repaid separately.

    Follow the instructions from your provincial loan provider to repay your loan. You can follow the instructions on this page for the federal part of your loan.

  • Quebec, Nunavut or the Northwest Territories

    You pay back your loan directly to your province or territory. Contact your provincial loan provider to arrange payments.

Increase or decrease your monthly payment

Above the minimum amount

The amount you pay over and above your minimum monthly payment is applied directly toward the principal amount of your loan. This decreases the total loan amount and the total interest you will have to pay.

  • Customize your monthly payment amount in your NSLSC account

If you increased your monthly payment above the minimum amount, you can decrease it back through your NSLSC account. You cannot reduce it below the minimum amount, unless you do a Revision of Terms.

Reducing your monthly payment below the minimum amount

You can ask to have the terms of your loan changed if you need to reduce your payments below the minimum amount.

With a Revision of Terms, you can to extend your repayment period from 114 months (9.5 years) to up to 174 months (14.5 years).

This will make your monthly payments smaller. However, you'll end up paying more interest in the end because it will take you longer to pay back your loan.

  • Contact the National Student Loans Service Centre (NSLSC) to find out if you can do a Revision of Terms

If you need more help in repaying your loan, go to Repayment assistance.

Payment date and frequency update

You can change the day of the month your payment is due:

  • Change Your Payment Due Date form (PDF, 84KB)

Fill out the form and mail it to:

National Student Loan Service Centre
P.O. Box 4030
Mississauga, ON
L5A 4M4

You can set up weekly or bi-weekly payments by adding multiple days of the month as payment dates.

Consider setting payments soon after your receive a pay check to help pay off your debt in the quickest way that works for you.

Update your banking information

Pre-authorized monthly payments will be taken out of your account automatically.

If you do not have an authorized bank account on your file or if you wish to change your banking information, fill out and submit the pre-authorized payments form:

  • Pre-Authorized Payment form (PDF, 128 KB)

Fill out the form and mail it to:

National Student Loan Service Centre
P.O. Box 4030
Mississauga, ON
L5A 4M4

Change your interest rate option

You can change your interest rate option from floating to fixed interest at any time by contacting the NSLSC. However, once you have chosen fixed interest, you cannot change back to floating interest.

  • Learn more about interest rate options
  • Contact the NSLSC

Loan repayment estimator

Use the Repayment estimator to to find out how much you would need to repay each month.

Make payments outside of monthly payments

There are other ways to make payments outside of your monthly payment schedule. You can:

  • Make a lump-sum payment at any time
  • Change to a different payment frequency, such as bi-weekly or weekly
