If you’re having trouble making payments, you can first try to reduce the amount you pay each month. If changing your payment doesn't help, you could get the following assistance.

Repayment Assistance Plan (RAP)

If you have a low income, the following plans can help you by reducing you monthly payment:

  • the Repayment Assistance Plan (RAP)
  • the Repayment Assistance Plan for Borrowers with a Permanent Disability (RAP-PD)

The RAP could reduce your monthly student loan payments. Depending on your financial situation, you may not even have to make any payments at all. If you have a permanent disability, the reduction would also take into account the expenses related to your permanent disability.

  • Find out if you can get help from the RAP and RAP-PD

Severe and permanent disability

If you have a severe and permanent disability, you may be able to cancel your student debt through the Severe Permanent Disability Benefit.

This can apply to you if you disability prevents you from participating in post-secondary studies and the labour force for the rest of your life

  • Apply for the Severe and Permanent Disability Benefit

Family doctors and nurses

If you are working as a family doctor, resident in family medicine, nurse or nurse practitioner in an under-served rural or remote community, you may be eligible for assistance to repay all or part of your student loans.

Over a period of 5 years, family doctors or residents in family medicine could receive up to $40,000 in loan forgiveness. Nurses or nurse practitioners could receive up to $20,000.

  • Apply for Canada Student Loan forgiveness for family doctors and nurses

Canadian Forces reservists

If you are a reservist in the Canadian Forces in a designated operation, you do not have to make payments on Canada Student Loans or provincial student loans.

  • Keep your loan interest free as a reservist
