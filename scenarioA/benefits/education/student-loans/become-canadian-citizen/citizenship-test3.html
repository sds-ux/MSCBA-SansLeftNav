Take the citizenship test and go to the interview

Who has to take the test, what’s on the test, how to study, what happens on test day.

Who has to take the test

You must take the citizenship test if you’re between the ages of 18 and 54 when you apply for citizenship.

If you haven't applied for citizenship, go to Apply for citizenship.

Study for the test

You can start studying for the test at any time.

What’s on the test

The citizenship test shows us what you know about Canada. We’ll ask you questions about the rights and responsibilities of being a Canadian citizen and Canada’s:

  • history
  • geography
  • economy
  • government
  • laws
  • symbols

The citizenship test does not have questions to assess your language skills in English or French.

A citizenship officer will note how well you communicate in one of the official languages during your interview.

How to study for the test

Use our official study guide to prepare for your test. We base all of the test questions on information found in this guide.

The study guide is always free.

The guide is available in multiple formats. You can choose to:

  • read the study guide online
  • listen to the MP3 version
  • download the PDF or eBook for offline viewing
  • order a paper copy of the study guide

After studying, you can take a practice test, similar to the real Canadian citizenship test.

When and where is the test

We’ll send you a notice with the date, time and location of your test. You'll get the notice about two weeks before the test. You should plan two hours for this activity.

If you aren’t available on the date and time of your appointment:

  • Email or write to the local office that sent you the notice explaining the reason why you are not available.
  • Use the online web form

Generally, once we receive your e-mail or letter, we’ll schedule your appointment on a different date.

If you don’t provide an explanation, or your explanation is not reasonable, we may stop processing your application and not grant you citizenship.

What happens on the day of the test

What to bring

When you come for your test, bring:

  • the original documents you sent with your application
    • as listed in the document checklist for your application
  • any passport or travel documents you used in the five years before you applied

Taking the test

You can take the test in English or French. You have 30 minutes to finish the test.

The test has 20 questions. There are multiple choice and true or false questions. You must answer at least 15 questions correctly.

Your test may be written or oral. We base the type of test on a number of things. For example, if you have trouble reading and writing in English or French, you will have an oral test.

An oral test is given by a citizenship officer through a hearing.

The interview

After the test, you’ll meet with a citizenship officer for an interview. During the interview the citizenship officer will:

  • give you the results of your test
  • note how well you communicate in English or French
  • verify your application and original documents
  • ask any questions we may have about your application
  • make sure you meet all the requirements for citizenship
  • inform you of decisions on your application

After the test and interview

If you pass and meet the other requirements for citizenship, we may:

  • give you a ceremony date at the same time we give you the test results or
  • email or send you a letter with the date and time of your ceremony

The ceremony will usually take place within three months after your test.

If you don’t pass your first written test, but meet the other requirements for citizenship, we will schedule you for a second test.

If you don’t pass your second test, we will send you a notice telling you to come to a hearing with a citizenship officer. The hearing:.

  • will last 30 to 90 minutes
  • may be in person or by videoconference
  • gives you the chance to show you meet the requirements, including:
    • language
    • knowledge of Canada

If we ask you to go to an interview, but you applied for citizenship with your family, we will process your application separately from your family's. If you don’t want to be processed separately, you can ask us to pause the rest of the applications.
